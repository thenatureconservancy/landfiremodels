A quick and quanititative ecosystem inventory of Ottawa National Forest

https://randy-swaty.shinyapps.io/OttawaAssessment/

National Forests have a complicated job: managing groups of complex ecosystems in a changing climate all while trying to keep a wide variety of stakeholders happy, often with very limited resources. Randy has been working with the Ottawa National Forest in the western Upper Peninsula of MI to complete a draft rapid assessment of their dominant ecosystems. We’re hoping this will assist them with prioritization and planning for future management.

Techniques and tools of potential interest include:

  • Maps made with open-sourced QGIS software. https://qgis.org/en/site/
  • Interactive charts developed with R-Shiny. https://shiny.rstudio.com/
  • Formatting achieved with the Tufte package for R. https://rstudio.github.io/tufte/
  • All spatial data is from the LANDFIRE program. www.landfire.gov

Thanks to Eric Rebitzke and the Ottawa National Forest for their support.

The Driftless Region of the U.S.: A Rapid Ecosystem Assessment

IN PROGRESS

The Driftless Region is a large and beguiling area of the midwestern United States that was missed by the glaciers, and of interest to Cole, Myles and Randy (and many others!). It’s topographically diverse, which has contributed to an interesting mosaic of ecosystems, ranging from riparian to very fire dependant prairies. Because of the location and habitat variability, the Driftless is predicted to have relativility high climate change resiliency (i.e., species there have options). Much of the area has been converted to agricultural and urban land uses. This coupled with disruption of natural processes such as fire have left the remaining natural vegetation compromised. Now what? Where should we look first for opportunities to restore and assist the ecosystems there? Our assessment is a quick look at the conditions of the ecosystems there.

Techniques and tools of potential interest include:

  • Maps made with open-sourced QGIS software. https://qgis.org/en/site/
  • We developed Sunburst and Chord diagrams to help readers explore vegetation change. For more information on those techniques see https://www.rdocumentation.org/packages/sunburstR/versions/2.1.3 and https://github.com/mattflor/chorddiag
  • All spatial data is from the LANDFIRE program. www.landfire.gov
